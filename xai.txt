                                              Abstract
          This report covers the baseline phase of our explainable healthcare risk prediction
      project. We implement ChronoFormer with time-aware attention, trained on sequences
      of condition codes and inter-event time gaps using the Synthea dataset (parquet). Planned
      next steps—attention rollout and saliency visualization—follow the project proposal (Febru-
      ary 12, 2026).


1    Name of Our Product
ChronoFormer is the product name for our binary mortality risk predictor: it maps a chrono-
logical sequence of clinical condition codes and inter-event time gaps (in days between
consecutive events) to a probability of the adverse outcome.


2    Domain of Application
    • Field: healthcare analytics and clinical risk stratification, including mortality pre-
      diction in this baseline; the same modeling paradigm extends to related tasks such as
      readmission risk when applied to other cohorts (as outlined in our proposal).
    • Baseline data: synthetic yet realistic Synthea records (Hugging Face dataset richardyoung/synthea-57
      parquet tables patients and conditions). Our proposal targets MIMIC-IV or eICU stan-
      dardized to the MEDS (Medical Event Data Standard) format—that remains the intended
      direction for later milestones; the baseline is deliberately fixed on a reproducible synthetic
      pipeline.
    • Transparency: high-stakes medical use requires model transparency; we plan to address
      this via attention-based explanations (rollout and saliency maps).


3    Motivation to Complete the Project
    • Clinical and societal value: identifying high-risk patients early supports follow-up plan-
      ning and resource allocation.
    • Research goal: combine temporal modeling in a transformer for EHR-style event
      sequences with explainable AI (XAI)—understanding how the model weights past visits
      relative to time intervals is essential for clinical trust.
    • Engineering goal: deliver reproducible PyTorch code, a public GitHub repository, and a
      documented predictive baseline before layering interpretability methods.


4    The Real-World Problem Our Product Solves
In practice, the task is to estimate the probability of an adverse outcome from the patient’s
recorded condition history and the timing between events. In this baseline, the label is derived

from the presence of a death date in the patient record. Such models support clinical decision
support, cohort studies, and quality analytics. The current limitation is training on synthetic
data; deployment to real EHRs requires external validation and careful bias analysis.


5     The Technical Problem Being Solved (The Black Box)
    • Input: a discrete sequence of event codes and inter-event intervals (days).
    • Model: a neural network with event and time embeddings and time-aware attention: a
      learnable bias depending on binned relative time is added to attention logits (aligned with
      the proposal idea of coupling time with scaled dot-product attention).
    • Why it is a “black box”: a transformer does not, by itself, yield an interpretable clinical
      narrative of which visits and gaps drove the prediction. The next project stage therefore
      targets Attention Rollout and saliency-based visualizations.


6     The Baseline Implementation
6.1    Data and labels
From patients: if DEATHDATE is present, label = 1; otherwise 0. From conditions: events
sorted by time; time_delta is the number of days between consecutive events for each patient
(zero for the first event). Split 80 / 10 / 10 (train / validation / test) with random_state=42
(approximately 460k / 57.5k / 57.5k patients in our run).

6.2    Preprocessing
A vocabulary maps raw codes to indices; PAD = 0, UNK = 1. Sequences are padded or truncated
to max_len = 256 (if longer, the last 256 events are kept). A boolean mask marks valid positions
for attention.

6.3    ChronoFormer architecture
                                                                                     √
    • TimeAwareAttention: Q, K, V from embeddings; attention scores = QK ⊤ / dk +
      time_bias (bias from embedding time bins), then softmax and context aggregation.
    • ChronoFormer: sum of event embedding and binned time embedding → one time-aware
      attention layer → LayerNorm → representation of the last sequence position → MLP with
      ReLU and a sigmoid output.

6.4    Training
Optimizer: AdamW. Loss: binary cross-entropy. Validation metrics: AUROC and F1 at thresh-
old 0.5. Best weights by validation AUROC are saved as chronoformer_best.pt; early stopping
with patience 5; up to 50 epochs. Config: batch_size = 128, d_model = 128, n_heads = 4,
lr = 5 × 10−5 . On CUDA: cudnn.benchmark and pin_memory; num_workers = 0 for stable Win-
dows/Jupyter behavior with large in-memory datasets.

6.5    Results (from training logs)
Validation AUROC increased from roughly 0.93 at epoch 0 to about 0.954+ by epochs 10–
11 (full log truncated). Insert the final test AUROC and F1 from the complete output of

8     Lessons Learned
     • Modeling temporal structure (not only code order but inter-event gaps) matches the
       nature of clinical trajectories.
     • End-to-end runs on hundreds of thousands of patients require attention to memory and
       preprocessing time; fixing random_state and saving checkpoints simplifies comparison with
       future XAI experiments.
     • It is important to separate classification performance from explanation quality in
       later stages (fidelity metrics and related checks, as in our proposal deliverables).
